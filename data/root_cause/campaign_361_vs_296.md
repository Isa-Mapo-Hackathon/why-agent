# Question
"Why does campaign 361 convert 60x better than campaign 296?"

# Root Cause

**Naive:** "361's emails are better."

**Agent's investigation:**

- Confirms the 60x gap
- Audience decomposition: 361 went to 65% eventually-converters vs 296 at 9% — most of the gap
- Subject decomposition: 361 uses a deadline cue, 296 mentions "sale" — small effect, not the main driver
- Timing decomposition: 361 sent June 11 — one day before Russia Day. 296 sent June 3 — no nearby holiday. The pre-holiday timing aligns with the retailer's standard practice for high-conversion sends.
- Cross-check: looks at other June campaigns. Campaign 297 (June 7, also high audience-quality) is not holiday-adjacent and converts at 0.75 per 1000. Campaign 361 (holiday-adjacent + high audience) converts at 8.07 per 1000. The gap suggests the holiday effect is real and substantial.

**Conclusion:** the 60x conversion gap is a combination of audience targeting (~70% of the gap), holiday timing (~25%), and subject features (~5%). Each factor is real, none alone explains it.
